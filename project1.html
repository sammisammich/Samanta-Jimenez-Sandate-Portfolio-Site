Who is Culturally Relevant Science (CRSci)?

Culturally Relevant Science is a nonprofit organization dedicated to creating innovative, affordable, and inclusive K–12 STEM curriculum for all students, especially those from underrepresented communities. They believe that every student, regardless of how they identify, deserves to see themselves represented in STEM, which helps increase engagement, achievement, and pursuit of STEM careers.

Overview

Role:
UX Designer
Team:
Develop for Good
Timeline:
13 weeks

Outcome: Improved curriculum accessibility for teachers and introduced structured analytics for admins, reducing friction in both content discovery and performance tracking.

The Problem

Culturally Relevant Science (CRSci) provides K–12 STEM curriculum, but all materials were stored in a large Google Drive system. This created significant friction for both teachers and administrators.

  • Teachers struggled to locate updated lesson materials
  • Content lacked organization and consistency
  • Admins had no visibility into student performance
  • Workflows were inefficient and time-consuming

As a result, users experienced delays, confusion, and increased manual effort.

Solution

We redesigned CRSci’s platform into a structured, role-based learning hub.

The new system enables teachers to quickly find and use curriculum materials while giving admins tools to monitor student performance and engagement.

My Role

  • Conducted user and stakeholder interviews
  • Mapped user journeys for teachers and admins
  • Designed wireframes and high-fidelity prototypes
  • Led usability testing and iterative improvements

Research

To understand user needs, we conducted interviews with teachers and administrators and analyzed existing educational platforms.

Key Insights

  • Ease of access is critical: Teachers need quick, reliable access to materials
  • Adaptability matters: Content must be flexible across classrooms
  • Data visibility is lacking: Admins need clear performance insights
  • Simplicity over complexity: Users prefer intuitive, streamlined systems

Competitive analysis revealed that while platforms like Khan Academy and Code.org provide strong content delivery, they often lack flexibility and customization for specialized curricula like CRSci.

Key Design Decisions

  • Structured curriculum library: Organized by grade and unit to reduce search time
  • Preview-first interaction: Allows teachers to quickly evaluate lessons before downloading
  • Role-based dashboards: Separate experiences for teachers and admins
  • Simplified analytics: Clear visual summaries instead of complex data tables

Understanding User Workflows

We mapped teacher and admin journeys to identify inefficiencies and opportunities for improvement.

Exploration & Wireframes

Early wireframes focused on simplifying navigation and validating information architecture before investing in visual design.

Final Design

The final interface prioritizes clarity, efficiency, and structured workflows.

Iteration

Usability testing revealed key areas for improvement:

  • File types needed clearer visual differentiation
  • Unit labels were unclear and inconsistent
  • Analytics pages were too complex

We refined hierarchy, labeling, and layout to improve clarity and usability.

Impact

  • Reduced time required to locate lesson materials
  • Improved clarity and consistency of curriculum organization
  • Enabled admins to track student performance effectively

The redesign provided CRSci with a scalable foundation for managing and delivering their curriculum more efficiently.

Reflection

This project strengthened my ability to design for real-world systems where organization, clarity, and efficiency are critical. Working with a nonprofit client introduced constraints that required balancing user needs with practical implementation, reinforcing the importance of designing solutions that are both usable and feasible.

I learned that improving user experience isn’t always about adding new features, but often about restructuring information and simplifying workflows. By focusing on how teachers and administrators actually navigate their tasks, we were able to reduce friction and create a more intuitive system.

Overall, this project reinforced my ability to translate complex, unstructured systems into clear, user-centered solutions and to design with both user needs and organizational constraints in mind.
