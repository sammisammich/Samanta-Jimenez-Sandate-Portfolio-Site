How I work

Design is how I advocate for my community, the undocumented, low-income, Spanish-speaking families whose struggles are written out of most design processes before they even begin.

I make things for people who don't usually see themselves in tech. That means doing the extra research, asking the uncomfortable questions, and refusing to let “general audience” be an excuse for exclusion.

Accessibility first
If it's not accessible, it's not finished. I design with people who are typically deprioritised at the centre, not bolted on at the end.
Research over assumptions
I never design for a community without first listening to them. Assumptions are where equity dies. Interviews, surveys, and lived experience come first.
Clarity is kindness
Complex language is a barrier. I design for comprehension, plain words, clear hierarchy, and interfaces that don't demand prior knowledge to navigate.
