ROAM: Encouraging Local Exploration

A scavenger-hunt–style mobile app designed to help users break routine, explore their communities, and support local businesses.

At a Glance

Project Type:
UX/UI Mobile App
Team:
7 Designers
Methods:
Survey, Personas, Testing
Focus:
Exploration + Social Play

The Problem

Boredom and monotonous lifestyles are increasing. Many people want to explore their cities but struggle to discover activities that match their interests. Planning outings requires switching between multiple apps, scrolling endlessly, and filtering unreliable recommendations.

Users often repeat the same 5–10 activities, feel overwhelmed by options, and lack motivation to try new local experiences — especially socially.

Target Audience

  • Young adults seeking new experiences
  • People new to an area
  • Social explorers & food/cafe enthusiasts
  • Families planning weekend activities

User Research & Surveys

Survey Size: 33 respondents

About 60% of respondents use Google Maps, Yelp, TikTok, or Instagram to discover activities — but none provide guided, interactive outings.

  • Users feel stuck in routine
  • Too many choices → decision fatigue
  • Hard to find truly local gems
  • Want social & shareable experiences
  • Want budget-aware planning tools

Personas

Mary Park — Student Explorer (Age 20)

  • Cafe hopping & social outings
  • Finds spots through TikTok
  • Feels bored with repeat places
  • Wants personalized local discovery

Competitive Analysis

We evaluated Yelp, Google Maps, TikTok, Beli, Let’s Roam, and others.

  • Strong at search & reviews
  • Weak in gamification
  • Limited social exploration
  • No guided real-world adventure flow

ROAM fills this gap with guided, gamified, social exploration.

Information Architecture

  • Home Dashboard
  • Create / Join Roam
  • Interactive Map
  • Feed (social posts)
  • Rewards & Points
  • Leaderboard
  • Profile & Settings
  • Invites & Notifications

Wireframes → Iterations

Low- and mid-fidelity wireframes explored scavenger hunt flows, map progress tracking, and social invitations.

Key early issues discovered:

  • Low feature discoverability
  • Roam creation confusion
  • Unclear navigation

Usability Testing Findings

  1. Low Feature Discoverability — users couldn’t find start actions
  2. Roam Creation Confusion — missing options & unclear labels
  3. Lack of System Feedback — users unsure if actions succeeded

Over 50% of participants said they strongly want tools that help them try new activities — validating the concept.

Branding & Design System

  • Warm exploration-focused palette
  • IBM Plex Sans typography system
  • Mascot + badge system
  • Gamified reward visuals

Final Prototype

  • Scavenger-hunt style roams
  • Personalized recommendations
  • Interactive map + progress tracking
  • Points & rewards system
  • Social feed & co-op hunts
  • Leaderboards & badges

Future Enhancements

  • Tiered rewards & streak bonuses
  • Co-op team hunts
  • Multi-day roam planning
  • Lodging & travel integrations
  • Weekly/monthly challenges

Reflection

This project reinforced the value of iteration and research-driven design. Each version improved clarity, feedback, and engagement. ROAM demonstrates how UX can transform everyday discovery into a playful, social experience.
