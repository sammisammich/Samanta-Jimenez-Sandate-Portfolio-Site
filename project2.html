Improving Health Insurance Literacy for UCI Students

A collaborative UX design project focused on making healthcare information more accessible and understandable for college students.

Project Overview

This project was completed as part of a class assignment in collaboration with six other students. Our goal was to design a digital solution that helps UC Irvine students better understand their health insurance options and make more informed healthcare decisions.

Through research, ideation, and design, we explored how simplified language, clear comparisons, and student-centered guidance could improve health insurance literacy and reduce confusion around coverage.

Problem Statement

Many college students at UC Irvine struggle to understand their health insurance options. While resources may exist, much of the information is difficult to access, filled with complex terminology, or scattered across multiple platforms.

This lack of clarity can lead to confusion, underutilization of available services, and unmet healthcare needs. Without an easy-to-use and reliable platform to improve healthcare knowledge, students may continue to make uninformed decisions that negatively impact both their well-being and financial stability.

Project Context

  • Who: College students
  • What: Understanding health insurance options
  • Where: UC Irvine

UX Process

Our competitive analysis revealed a consistent pattern across existing health insurance platforms: most assume users already understand insurance basics or push them toward enrollment before they feel confident. These gaps raised an important question— how do college students actually experience and understand health insurance today?

To validate these observations and ensure our design addressed real student needs, we conducted research focused on students’ insurance literacy, confidence, and decision-making behaviors when navigating healthcare systems.

Research

Building on insights from our competitive analysis, our research aimed to better understand how students currently engage with health insurance information, where breakdowns occur, and what prevents them from confidently using existing platforms.

Research Questions

  • What baseline understanding do college students have about health insurance?
  • Which insurance terms, concepts, or plan details cause the most confusion?
  • How do students currently seek out health insurance information?
  • At what points do students feel overwhelmed or disengage from existing platforms?
  • What type of guidance helps students feel informed without feeling pressured to commit?

Our findings revealed that healthcare information is often inaccessible to students due to confusing terminology, dense explanations, and assumptions of prior knowledge—issues that closely mirrored patterns identified in our competitive analysis. Many students reported feeling overwhelmed by the nuances of insurance plans and unsure how to determine which option best fits their needs.

This lack of clarity frequently led to hesitation and avoidance. Students expressed discomfort when platforms requested personal information early in the process, reinforcing our observation that many tools prioritize enrollment over education.

Scholarly research further supports these findings. One study on health insurance literacy among college students found that, despite high rates of insurance coverage, students often report low confidence and understanding when selecting or using their plans.

“Approximately half of respondents (50.8%) indicated that they had been confused about selecting or using their health insurance plan, and nearly one-quarter reported delaying or avoiding medical care due to this confusion.”

Design Implications

  • Allow students to explore and learn without providing personal information upfront.
  • Break down insurance concepts into clear, student-friendly categories.
  • Use progressive disclosure to prevent information overload.

User Flow

To translate research insights into a clear product structure, we designed a user flow that supports students from initial uncertainty to confident decision-making. The flow reflects our research findings and prioritizes education, reduced cognitive load, and guidance without pressure.

User Flow Description

This project explored how UX design can improve health insurance literacy for UC Irvine students.

Our goal was to design an educational, low-pressure digital experience that helps students understand insurance terminology, compare plan options, and make informed healthcare decisions without feeling overwhelmed or rushed into enrollment.

Key Design Principles Behind the Flow

  • Education before personalization to reduce anxiety and disengagement.
  • Minimal data collection to avoid premature commitment.
  • Progressive disclosure to prevent information overload.
  • A clear handoff to official providers for enrollment.

Storyboards

Before designing individual screens, we created storyboards to visualize how students might move through the platform over time. These storyboards helped us focus on the overall journey—from learning unfamiliar insurance terminology to comparing plan options—without getting constrained by layout or visual details.

Using insights from our research and user flow, the storyboards allowed us to explore pacing, guidance, and moments of potential confusion. They ultimately served as the foundation for our high-fidelity prototypes.

Early Storyboard Explorations

We explored multiple storyboard flows to understand how different entry points and levels of guidance could affect student confidence and engagement.

Prototyping

We developed mid- to high-fidelity prototypes that emphasized step-by-step guidance, simplified explanations, and visual hierarchy. The design aimed to reduce cognitive load and help students feel more confident navigating their healthcare choices.

User Testing

User testing helped us identify areas where terminology was still unclear or where users needed additional context. Based on feedback, we refined language, improved navigation clarity, and adjusted content structure to better support student understanding.

Final Design

The final design presents health insurance information in a more approachable and student-friendly format. By focusing on clarity, accessibility, and guidance, the solution helps students feel more confident when selecting and using their health insurance.

Results & Takeaways

This project highlighted the importance of designing for health literacy and accessibility. Working in a team environment strengthened my collaboration skills and reinforced the value of user-centered design when addressing complex, real-world problems.

The experience emphasized how thoughtful UX decisions can empower users to make better, more informed choices—especially in areas that directly impact their well-being.
